# No-go: a hyperbola cannot be the plinth divisor of an LND on affine 3-space

Let `k` be algebraically closed of characteristic zero and let

\[
B=k[x,y,z],\qquad 0\ne\delta\in\operatorname{LND}_k(B),\qquad
A=\ker\delta=k[u,v].
\]

Then it is impossible that

\[
\operatorname{pl}(\delta):=A\cap\delta(B)=(uv-1).
\]

Consequently, there cannot be a minimal local slice `r` with
`delta(r)=uv-1`. In particular, the proposed four-variable derivation

\[
\Delta=\delta+u\partial_w
\]

cannot acquire the slice `S=-r+vw` by this construction (formally one
would have `Delta(S)=-(uv-1)+uv=1`, but the required three-variable
`delta` does not exist).

## Proof over the complex numbers

For a locally nilpotent derivation on `C^[3]`, put

\[
I_1=(\ker\delta)\cap\delta(B).
\]

Freudenburg's formulation of Kaliman's theorem says: if
`Y=Spec(ker delta) \cong A^2` and `C \subset Y` is the curve defined by
`I_1`, then every irreducible component of `C` is a *polynomial curve*,
i.e. its normalization is `A^1`.  See:

- S. Kaliman, *Free C+-actions on C3 are translations*, Invent. Math.
  156 (2004), Theorem 5.2 (Theorem 5.4 in the arXiv source);
- G. Freudenburg, *Canonical factorization of the quotient morphism for
  an affine Ga-variety*, arXiv:1602.08786, Section 7, items (c) and (e).
  Item (c) identifies `I_1` with the plinth ideal, and item (e) applies
  the polynomial-curve conclusion to the curve defined by `I_1`.

If `I_1=(uv-1)`, this curve is irreducible and

\[
C=V(uv-1)\cong\mathbb G_m,
\qquad \mathbb C[C]=\mathbb C[u,u^{-1}].
\]

It is already normal, and its normalization is `G_m`, not `A^1`.  An
equally elementary way to see that it is not a polynomial curve is that
every morphism `A^1 -> G_m` is constant: the image of the invertible
coordinate `u` must belong to `C[t]^*=C^*`.  Thus no dominant polynomial
parametrization exists. This contradicts Kaliman's theorem.

## Descent from an arbitrary algebraically closed characteristic-zero field

Assume such data exist over `k`. Choose a finitely generated subfield
`K \subset k` containing all coefficients occurring in `delta(x)`,
`delta(y)`, `delta(z)`, `u`, `v`, and a local slice `r` satisfying
`delta(r)=uv-1`. Write `B_K=K[x,y,z]` and let `delta_K` be the descended
derivation.

The following base-change identities hold for every field extension
`L/K`:

\[
\ker(\delta_K)\otimes_K L=\ker(\delta_L),
\qquad
\ker(\delta_K^2)\otimes_K L=\ker(\delta_L^2),
\]

because tensoring vector spaces with a field is exact. Moreover

\[
\operatorname{pl}(\delta_K)
=\delta_K(\ker\delta_K^2).
\]

Indeed, `delta(b)` is invariant exactly when `delta^2(b)=0`. Hence the
plinth ideal also commutes with field extension, as the image of the
displayed linear map. Faithful flatness, together with the assumed
identities over `k`, gives

\[
\ker\delta_K=K[u,v],\qquad
\operatorname{pl}(\delta_K)=(uv-1).
\]

(For the first equality, one can equivalently intersect
`k[u,v]` with `K[x,y,z]`; algebraic independence of `u,v` and faithful
flatness give exactly `K[u,v]`.)

Since `K` is finitely generated over `Q`, it embeds into `C`. After
base change along an embedding `K -> C`, the same two equalities hold
over `C`, contradicting the complex case. This proves the assertion for
all algebraically closed fields of characteristic zero.

## Wider usable obstruction

More generally, if the principal plinth ideal of an LND on `k^[3]` is
generated by

\[
a=\prod_i a_i^{e_i}\in k[u,v],
\]

then every irreducible plane curve `V(a_i)` has normalization `A^1`.
Thus no irreducible factor may have positive genus, normalization
`G_m`, or two or more places at infinity. In particular every
`uv-c` with `c != 0`, in any coordinate system `(u,v)` on the kernel,
is excluded.

There is a stronger obstruction tailored to the proposed four-variable
template. Suppose `u` is a variable of the kernel `A=k[u,v]`, `r` is a
minimal local slice, and

\[
\delta(r)=a=u h-1\qquad(h\in A).
\]

Then every irreducible factor `q` of `a` defines a polynomial curve by
Kaliman's theorem. In the domain `A/(q)` one has `u h=1`, so `u` is a
unit. Pulling it back to the normalization `k[t]` of `V(q)`, it becomes
a unit of `k[t]`, hence a constant `c\in k^*`. Since
`A/(q) \hookrightarrow k[t]`, already `u=c` in `A/(q)`. Thus
`q | (u-c)`, and irreducibility forces `q` to be associated to `u-c`.
This holds for every irreducible factor of `a`, so

\[
a\in k[u].
\]

A standard local-slice lemma for LNDs on `k^[3]` now says that if `u` is
a variable of `ker(delta)` and `delta(r)\in k[u]`, then `u` is a variable
of the ambient `k^[3]`. Hence `rank(delta)<=2`. (See Dasgupta--Gaifullin,
arXiv:2306.00510, Lemma 2.15(ii), and Proposition 5.3 for precisely this
rank consequence.) Therefore:

> No rank-three LND on `k^[3]` can have a minimal plinth generator of
> the form `u h-1` with `u` a kernel coordinate and `h` any invariant.

So the obstruction is not special to choosing the second kernel
coordinate `h=v`: the entire affine-linear slice ansatz
`Delta=delta+u partial_w`, `S=-r+h w` is ruled out when `r` is minimal.
