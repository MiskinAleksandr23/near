# A finite formal-neighborhood criterion for polynomial cancellation

Let `k` be a field of characteristic zero,

```text
R=k[x_1,...,x_m]=B[f],
```

and let `D` be the `B`-derivation determined by `D(f)=1`.  Let
`rho:R -> B` be specialization at `f=0`.  Since `ker(rho)=(f)`, there
are unique polynomials

```text
h_i=(x_i-rho(x_i))/f in R.                              (1)
```

Thus `x=rho(x)+f h`.  This is the first formal displacement of the
retraction.

## The invariant-subspace criterion

**Proposition.**  Suppose that, after an affine linear change of the
ambient coordinates, the variables can be split as

```text
x=(u_1,...,u_r,v_1,...,v_q),       r+q=m, q>=1,
```

in such a way that

```text
h_{u_i}=0,                         1<=i<=r,
h_{v_j} in A:=k[u_1,...,u_r],      1<=j<=q.              (2)
```

Then `f` is a coordinate of `R`, and `B=k^[m-1]`.

The condition is finite and directly checkable from `rho`: compute the
quotients (1), find a linear subspace of ambient coordinates on which
they vanish, and test whether all remaining quotients belong to the
polynomial ring generated by that subspace.

**Proof.**  Equation (2) first gives `rho(u_i)=u_i`, hence `u_i in B`
and `A subset B`.  It also gives

```text
v_j=rho(v_j)+f h_{v_j}(u).
```

Applying `D` shows

```text
D(u_i)=0,             D(v_j)=h_{v_j}(u).                 (3)
```

In particular the first-order expansion is already the entire
`f`-adic expansion (`D^2(x_i)=0` for every ambient variable).

By the chain rule and `D(f)=1`,

```text
1=sum_j f_{v_j} h_{v_j}(u).
```

Consequently the row `h=(h_{v_1},...,h_{v_q})` is unimodular over
`A`: if its entries generated a proper ideal of `A`, a maximal ideal
containing it would remain proper after extension to the polynomial
ring `R=A[v_1,...,v_q]`, contradicting the displayed identity.  Choose
`a_1,...,a_q in A` with

```text
sum_j a_j h_{v_j}=1.                                    (4)
```

The row `a=(a_1,...,a_q)` is itself unimodular by (4).  Since `A` is a
polynomial ring over a field, Quillen--Suslin (equivalently, completion
of unimodular rows over `A`) supplies a matrix `U in GL_q(A)` whose
first row is `a`.  Therefore the `A`-linear change

```text
(v'_1,...,v'_q)^t=U(v_1,...,v_q)^t
```

is a polynomial automorphism of the ambient ring.  Its first coordinate

```text
s=v'_1=sum_j a_j(u)v_j
```

satisfies `D(s)=1` by (3)--(4).  Hence `s-f in ker(D)=B`, and

```text
R=B[f]=B[s].
```

As `s` is an ambient coordinate, specializing at `s=0` now gives

```text
B ~= R/(s) ~= k^[m-1].
```

This also proves that `f` is a coordinate.  QED.

The proposition is not a disguised form of the general coordinate
problem: it constructs the required ambient coordinate `s` by a finite
unimodular-row computation in the known polynomial subring `A`.

## A degree-six example outside the quadratic and graded classes

In `R=k[x,y,z]`, put

```text
s=z-xy,
u=y-xz+x^2 y.
```

The change of variables is polynomial, since

```text
y=u+xs,
z=(1+x^2)s+xu.
```

Thus `R=k[x,u,s]`.  Set

```text
B=k[x,u],
f=s+x^3+u^2.                                             (5)
```

Then `R=B[f]`, while `deg_(x,y,z)(f)=6`.  The derivation
`D=partial/partial s` in the coordinates `(x,u,s)` has

```text
D(x)=0,        D(y)=x,        D(z)=1+x^2.                 (6)
```

For the retraction at `f=0`, formula (1) consequently gives

```text
h=(0,x,1+x^2).
```

The criterion applies with `A=k[x]`.  A Bezout identity is

```text
(-x)*x + 1*(1+x^2)=1,
```

and its constructed ambient slice is exactly

```text
(-x)y+z=s.
```

This example is not made positive weighted-homogeneous by any affine
change of the original coordinates.  Here is a compact coefficient
certificate.  If an affine change made `f-c` weighted-homogeneous,
pulling back its Euler field would give a nonzero affine derivation

```text
E=(M(x,y,z)^t+c_0) dot (partial_x,partial_y,partial_z)
```

and constants `lambda,mu`, with `lambda!=0`, such that

```text
E(f)=lambda f+mu.                                        (7)
```

Write the fourteen unknowns in the order

```text
M_11,M_12,M_13,M_21,M_22,M_23,M_31,M_32,M_33,
c_1,c_2,c_3,lambda,mu.
```

For the expanded polynomial

```text
f=x^4y^2-2x^3yz+x^2z^2+2x^2y^2-2xyz+y^2+x^3-xy+z,
```

the coefficients in (7) of the following fourteen monomials form a
square matrix:

```text
x^3y^2z, x^3y^3, x^4yz, x^4y^2, x^5y,
x^3yz, x^3y^2, x^4y, x^2yz, x^2y^2,
x^3y, x^2y, x^3, 1.
```

Its determinant is `-32768`.  Thus (7) has only the zero solution in
characteristic zero, a contradiction.  So (5) lies outside both the
`deg(f)<=2` class and the positive-grading-after-affine-change class.

## A false intermediate lemma exposed by the same example

It is tempting to hope that a linear formal expansion plus a very
nilpotent Jacobian already exposes a constant ambient direction.  This
is false.  In (6),

```text
D^2(x)=D^2(y)=D^2(z)=0,
h=(0,x,1+x^2),
(Jac h)^2=0,
(h_1,h_2,h_3)R=R.
```

Nevertheless no constant vector `c in k^3` has
`c dot h in k*`: a polynomial

```text
a x+b(1+x^2)
```

can be constant only when `a=b=0`.  Thus neither `D^2(x_i)=0` nor
square-zero `Jac(h)`, even together with unimodularity and a slice,
forces a constant directional derivative.  The proposition succeeds
because it uses polynomial (not constant) unimodular completion over
the invariant subring `k[x]`.

## Formal/Jacobian identities worth retaining

For arbitrary `rho`, differentiating `x=rho(x)+fh` gives

```text
I=Jac(rho)+h (grad f)^t+f Jac(h).                         (8)
```

On the section `f=0`, this says that `Jac(rho)` and
`h (grad f)^t` are complementary projectors, because
`(grad f)^t h=1` there.  Under (2), the last term of (8) is already
controlled over `A`, and the Bezout completion above is the precise
algebraization step.  Without the invariant-subspace hypothesis, even
`(Jac h)^2=0` does not provide that step, as the example shows.
