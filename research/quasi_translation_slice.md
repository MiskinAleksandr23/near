# Slice quasi-translations on `k^[4]`

Let `k` be a field of characteristic zero, let

```text
R = k[x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4],
D = sum_i H_i partial_{x_i},
```

and assume

```text
D^2(x_i)=0 for every i,                 (QT)
D(s)=1 for some s in R.                 (S)
```

Thus every `H_i=D(x_i)` is invariant, `D` is locally nilpotent, and
`x -> x+tH(x)` is a quasi-translation.  Condition (S) also implies

```text
(H_1,H_2,H_3,H_4)R=R.                  (1)
```

This note proves two genuinely rectifiable subclasses and gives a rank-two
example which rules out two tempting linear versions of the argument.

## 1. A completely elementary linear-span theorem

Write `span(H)` for the linear span of the image of the polynomial map `H`,
or equivalently for the span of its coefficient vectors in `k^4`.

**Proposition 1.**  If `dim span(H)<=2`, then `D` is conjugate by a polynomial
automorphism of `R` to a standard translation.

**Proof.**  A constant linear change makes all but at most two components of
`H` zero.  The one-component case follows immediately from (1), so consider

```text
R=A[u,v],                 A=k[z_1,z_2],
D|_A=0,                   D(u)=p,  D(v)=q.              (2)
```

Put `K=Frac(A)` and work first in `K[u,v]`.  The given `s` remains a slice.
By the slice theorem and cancellation in dimension one (equivalently, the
two-variable triangularization theorem for LNDs), there is a `t` such that

```text
K[u,v]=K[t,s],            D(t)=0,       D=partial_s.
```

Condition (QT) says that `u` and `v` have degree at most one in `s`, hence

```text
u=a(t)s+b(t),             v=c(t)s+d(t),                (3)
```

with `a,b,c,d in K[t]`.  Since `(t,s)->(u,v)` is a polynomial automorphism,
its Jacobian is a nonzero element of `K`.  Formula (3) gives

```text
J=(a'c-ac')s+(b'c-ad') in K*.                           (4)
```

Thus `a'c-ac'=0`.  If one of `a,c` is zero, the constant term in (4) makes
the other one a nonzero element of `K`.  If both are nonzero, then
`(a/c)'=0`, so `a=lambda c` for `lambda in K*`; the constant term in (4)
then makes `c`, and hence `a`, a nonzero element of `K`.  In every case

```text
p=D(u)=a in K,            q=D(v)=c in K.
```

But `p,q` originally belong to `A[u,v]`, so

```text
p,q in K intersect A[u,v]=A.                            (5)
```

Now (1) and faithful flatness of `A -> A[u,v]` show that `(p,q)A=A`.
Choose `alpha,beta in A` with `alpha p+beta q=1`.  The explicit matrix

```text
U = [ alpha   beta ]
    [  -q      p  ]       in SL_2(A)                    (6)
```

defines an ambient polynomial automorphism, fixing `A`, by

```text
y=alpha u+beta v,         w=-q u+p v.
```

It satisfies `D(y)=1` and `D(w)=0`.  Hence in the ambient coordinate system
`(z_1,z_2,w,y)` one has `D=partial_y`.  Notice that (6) is completed over the
known polynomial coordinate subring `A`, not over the unknown kernel. QED.

The same proof works in any dimension whenever the coefficient-span of `H`
has dimension at most two.

## 2. Jacobian rank at most one

We use the standard rank-one classification of quasi-translations.  In the
form needed here it says:

> If `rank Jac(H)=1`, choose a constant linear coordinate system in which
> `H_1=...=H_r=0` and the remaining components are linearly independent over
> `k`.  Then `r>=1` and every component of `H` belongs to
> `k[x_1,...,x_r]`.

This is Theorem 5.5 of M. de Bondt, *Quasi-translations and singular
Hessians* (the paper also notes that the statements work over arbitrary
characteristic-zero fields): <https://arxiv.org/abs/1501.05168>.

**Proposition 2.**  If `rank Jac(H)<=1`, then `D` is conjugate to a standard
translation.

**Proof.**  Rank zero means that `H` is a nonzero constant vector, and is
immediate.  In rank one apply the quoted classification and write

```text
A=k[x_1,...,x_r],         x=(x_1,...,x_r,v_1,...,v_q),
D(x_i)=0,                 D(v_j)=h_j in A.              (7)
```

The slice equation and (7) imply `(h_1,...,h_q)A=A`: otherwise a maximal
ideal of `A` containing the `h_j` would remain proper after adjoining the
`v_j`, contradicting `D(s)=1`.  Choose `a_j in A` with

```text
sum_j a_j h_j=1.                                          (8)
```

The row `(a_j)` is unimodular.  Quillen--Suslin completes it to a matrix
`U in GL_q(A)`.  The ambient automorphism

```text
(x,v) -> (x,U(x)v)
```

has first new `v`-coordinate `y=sum_j a_jv_j`, and (8) gives `D(y)=1`.
All the other rows can be altered by subtracting their `D`-value times `y`,
or, equivalently, one can choose a matrix carrying the unimodular column
`h` to the first basis column.  This yields an ambient coordinate system in
which `D=partial_y`. QED.

There is no hidden cancellation step here: the only unimodular completion
is over the explicitly exhibited polynomial ring `A` generated by ambient
linear invariant coordinates.

## 3. Rank two does not force an affine slice or the finite formal criterion

The next example is already a conjugated translation, but in the displayed
standard coordinates it has Jacobian rank two, no affine-linear slice, and
does not satisfy the invariant-subspace criterion after any affine linear
change.

In `R=k[x,y,z,w]` put

```text
s=z-xy,
u=y-xz+x^2y.                                             (9)
```

These are coordinates together with `x`, because

```text
y=u+xs,               z=(1+x^2)s+xu.                    (10)
```

Define, in the coordinate system `(x,u,s,w)`,

```text
D(x)=D(u)=0,          D(s)=1,          D(w)=u.           (11)
```

In the original coordinates this is

```text
D(x)=0,
D(y)=x,
D(z)=1+x^2,
D(w)=u=y-xz+x^2y.                                      (12)
```

All four coefficients in (12) are invariant, so `D^2` annihilates every
original coordinate.  Moreover

```text
k(Dx,Dy,Dz,Dw)=k(x,u),
```

and `x,u` are algebraically independent by (10).  Therefore
`rank Jac(H)=2`.

There is no affine-linear slice in the original coordinates.  Indeed, for
`ell=c_0+c_xx+c_yy+c_zz+c_ww`,

```text
D(ell)=c_y x+c_z(1+x^2)+c_w u.                           (13)
```

Algebraic independence of `x,u` shows that (13) cannot equal `1`.  The same
calculation shows that the only linear invariants are multiples of `x`.
Consequently, after any affine linear change the maximal linear invariant
coordinate subring is only `k[x]`, while the coefficient `D(w)=u` does not
belong to `k[x]`.  Thus the invariant-subspace hypothesis of
`formal_retraction_criterion.md` genuinely fails.

Nevertheless rectification is explicit: set

```text
W=w-us.
```

Then `(x,u,W,s)` is an ambient polynomial coordinate system and

```text
D(x)=D(u)=D(W)=0,       D(s)=1.
```

Thus this is a strict counterexample to both natural intermediate claims

```text
rank Jac(H)<=2  =>  existence of an affine-linear slice,
rank Jac(H)<=2  =>  applicability of the affine invariant-subspace test.
```

It is not a counterexample to rectifiability itself.

## 4. Slice does not force coefficient-span at most two

There is also a rank-two slice example whose coefficient-span is all of
`k^4`.  Put

```text
f=x_1+x_2x_4-x_3^2,
L=f^2x_2-2fx_3+x_4,
H=(-L,1,f,f^2).                                         (14)
```

For the derivation `D=sum H_i partial_{x_i}`, direct calculation gives

```text
D(f)=-L+x_4+x_2f^2-2x_3f=0,
D(L)=f^2-2f^2+f^2=0.
```

Thus `D(H_i)=0` for every `i`, while `D(x_2)=1`.  Hence (14) is a slice
quasi-translation and is already rectified by the ambient coordinate `x_2`.
Furthermore

```text
k(H_1,H_2,H_3,H_4)=k(f,L).
```

The polynomials `f,L` are algebraically independent: setting `x_2=x_3=0`
turns them into `x_1,x_4`.  Therefore `rank Jac(H)=2`.  Finally a constant
linear relation among the four components would have the form

```text
-c_1L+c_2+c_3f+c_4f^2=0.
```

The same specialization turns this into
`-c_1x_4+c_2+c_3x_1+c_4x_1^2=0`, so all `c_i` vanish.  Thus

```text
dim span(H)=4.                                           (15)
```

This decisively rules out the proposed implication

```text
slice + rank Jac(H)<=2 + dimension four
    => dim span(H)<=2 (or even <=3).
```

The example is the primitive version of the nonhomogeneous example in
de Bondt's paper, but all assertions needed here have been checked directly.

## 5. Homogenization: a new sufficient condition and an exact obstruction

Let `d=max deg H_i`, introduce `x_5`, and homogenize by

```text
Htilde(x,x_5)=x_5^d (H(x/x_5),0).                        (16)
```

Then `X+Htilde` is a homogeneous quasi-translation in dimension five and

```text
rank Jac(H) <= rank Jac(Htilde) <= rank Jac(H)+1.        (17)
```

For completeness, (QT) for (16) follows after factoring
`x_5^(2d-1)` from `Jac(Htilde) Htilde`; (17) follows from transcendence
degree after setting `x_5=1` and adjoining `x_5^d`.

**Proposition 3.**  Suppose `rank Jac(H)=2` and the homogenized map in (16)
still has Jacobian rank two.  Then `D` is conjugate to a translation.

**Proof.**  The Gordan--Noether rank-two theorem for homogeneous
quasi-translations is often stated for the primitive (called
"irreducible") vector.  We therefore include the reduction.  Let

```text
g=gcd(Htilde_1,...,Htilde_5),       Htilde=g K.
```

The standard primitive-reduction lemma for quasi-translations says that
`X+K` is again a homogeneous quasi-translation and `g` is an invariant of
it.  (It follows directly from `Htilde(X+tHtilde)=Htilde(X)` by cancelling
`g` and comparing quasi-degrees.)  Multiplication by the nonzero polynomial
`g` does not change constant linear relations among the components, so

```text
span(Htilde)=span(K).                                     (19)
```

The primitive rank cannot increase.  Indeed, write any polynomial relation
`R` as the sum of its ordinary homogeneous parts `R_m`.  Since all components
of `Htilde` have one common degree and all components of `K` have one common
degree,

```text
R(Htilde)=0  iff  R_m(K)=0 for every m  iff  R(K)=0.
```

(If `K` has degree zero its rank is already zero.)  Thus `K` and `Htilde`
have the same algebraic relations, hence the same Jacobian rank in
characteristic zero; in particular it is at most two.  If this rank is at
most one, the homogeneous
rank-one classification makes `K` a polynomial multiple of one constant
vector, so its coefficient-span has dimension at most one.  If the rank is
two, the Gordan--Noether theorem in its primitive form says that the linear
span of the image of a homogeneous rank-two quasi-translation in dimension
`N` has dimension at most `N-2`.  Thus for `N=5`, in either case, (19) gives
`dim span(Htilde)<=3`.  Evaluating constant linear relations at `x_5=1`
shows

```text
dim span(H)<=3.
```

After a constant linear change, at least one ambient coordinate is killed
by `D`.  Thus this LND has rank at most three.  The rank-at-most-three slice
theorem on `k^[4]` gives polynomial kernel and rectification (this is the
rank dichotomy recorded in `full_rank_slice_notes.md`). QED.

There is a useful geometric reading of the extra hypothesis.  Let

```text
W=closure(image(H)) subset A^4.
```

Since `rank Jac(H)=2`, `dim W=2`.  On `x_5!=0`, the image of (16) consists
densely of all scalar multiples of points of `image(H)` (every nonzero
scalar has a `d`-th root after passing to the algebraic closure).  Hence

```text
closure(image(Htilde)) = affine cone generated by W.    (18)
```

Consequently the rank in (17) stays two exactly when the conical hull in
(18) has dimension two; it jumps to three exactly when scalar dilation adds
one dimension.  In particular a still-unsettled rank-two candidate must
have a nonconical coefficient-image surface.

Example (14) exhibits the jump: its image lies in the affine hyperplane
`H_2=1` and has dimension two, while taking scalar multiples adds a third
dimension.  Thus the nonhomogeneous hypothesis cannot simply be discarded.

## 6. Exact remaining frontier

The preceding arguments settle:

1. every slice quasi-translation with `rank Jac(H)<=1`;
2. every slice quasi-translation whose coefficient-span has dimension at
   most two, irrespective of Jacobian rank.

At Jacobian rank two, neither an affine slice nor a linear-invariant-subspace
normal form follows, as Section 3 shows.  A proof for all rank-two slice
quasi-translations would therefore require a nonlinear invariant coordinate
mechanism (as `(x,u)` in (9)), not merely a completion of the coefficient row
or a constant linear conjugation.  Combining Sections 4 and 5, any genuinely
unsettled rank-two case must simultaneously satisfy all three conditions

```text
dim span(H)=4                 (no ambient linear invariant),
1 notin span_k{H_1,...,H_4}  (no affine-linear slice),
rank Jac(Htilde)=3            (the coefficient image is nonconical).
```

Example (14) satisfies the first and third conditions but not the second;
the example of Section 3 satisfies the second and third but not the first.
Thus the first two obstructions are logically independent in explicit
rectified examples, while the necessity of the third is Proposition 3.

There is one more exact homogeneous constraint coming from the slice.  Let

```text
I=(Htilde_1,...,Htilde_4) subset k[x_1,...,x_4,x_5].
```

The affine Bezout identity `(H_1,...,H_4)R=R` can be homogenized to a common
degree: for some `M` there are homogeneous `A_i` such that

```text
sum_i A_i Htilde_i = x_5^M.                              (20)
```

Consequently

```text
x_5 in radical(I),
V(I) subset {x_5=0}.                                    (21)
```

Conversely, an affine-linear slice is exactly a constant row `(c_i)` with
`sum c_iH_i=1`, and after homogenization this is exactly

```text
sum_i c_i Htilde_i=x_5^d.                               (22)
```

Thus the homogenization of every genuinely difficult rank-two case is a
very special homogeneous rank-three
quasi-translation in dimension five:

```text
Htilde_5=0;
Htilde_1,...,Htilde_4 are k-linearly independent;
x_5^M lies in their polynomial ideal for some M;
x_5^d does not lie in their constant linear span.
```

In geometric terms it has exactly the evident linear invariant `x_5`, and
the whole projective base locus of its first four components is at infinity.
General homogeneous rank-three quasi-translations in dimension five are not
covered by the rank-two Gordan--Noether theorem.  Therefore replacing the
last display by a claim of a second linear invariant would be precisely the
new missing lemma, not a consequence of the cited classification.
