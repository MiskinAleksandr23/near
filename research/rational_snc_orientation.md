# Constant-node orientation for a rational SNC coefficient divisor

Let

```text
f=a(y_1,y_2)x+G(y_1,y_2,w),   k[x,y_1,y_2,w]=B[f],
```

and suppose `D=V(a)_red` is a connected SNC union of embedded affine
lines `D_i`, with no triple points.  Multiplicities in `a` are arbitrary.
For an edge `s in D_i intersect D_j`, put `h_s(w)=G(s,w)`.

This note records a graph-theoretic conclusion that does not require the
components to form a rectangular arrangement.

## Proposition (orientation of constant nodes)

For a generic level `c`, write

```text
C_i=V(G|_(D_i x A1_w)-c),
delta_i=1-chi_c(C_i).
```

Call `D_i` inactive if its coordinate `t_i:D_i=A1` is constant on the
generic curve `C_i`.  Then:

1. an inactive restriction is exactly `lambda_i t_i+mu_i`, and every
   incident edge is constant;
2. no two inactive vertices are adjacent;
3. every constant edge has exactly one inactive endpoint;
4. every nonconstant edge has degree one;
5. if an active vertex has `r_i` inactive neighbors, then
   `delta_i=r_i`; its generic curve is rational with exactly `r_i+1`
   places at infinity.

Thus the constant-edge subgraph is oriented from an independent set of
inactive vertices toward active vertices, and contains no active-active
or inactive-inactive edge.

## Proof

At a generic level all component curves are smooth irreducible: the
level avoids every constant node value, so the UFD valuation comparison
has no shared boundary prime.  Stratifying the cancellation hypersurface
over `A2-D` and `D` gives

```text
chi(Y_c)=chi(D)=#V-#E.
```

Only nonconstant edges contribute intersections at a generic level.
Consequently

```text
sum_i delta_i + sum_(e nonconstant)(deg h_e-1)
   = # {constant edges}.                              (1)
```

If `t_i` is constant on `C_i`, irreducibility says the generic equation
on `D_i x A1` is a single fiber of `t_i`.  Varying the generic level
shows that the restriction of `G` factors through `t_i`; generic
irreducibility then makes that one-variable polynomial affine-linear.
This proves (1) of the proposition.

If two adjacent vertices were inactive, take their common constant value
`gamma`.  At level `gamma`, the divisor of each of the two component
equations is supported only on the same shared height-one prime.  But the
localization exact sequence identifies the free lattice generated by all
reduced components of `D` with the free lattice on all boundary primes:
both the cancellation ring and its localization are UFDs and their unit
quotient is exactly the component lattice.  Two rows supported on one
column contradict injectivity.  This proves (2).

Suppose an active vertex is incident with `r_i` constant edges.  On its
generic curve, the `r_i` functions `t_i-t_i(s)` are independent units
modulo constants.  For a smooth affine curve of genus `g` with `q`
punctures, the unit rank is at most `q-1`, while
`delta_i=2g+q-1`.  Hence

```text
delta_i>=r_i.                                         (2)
```

Every constant edge incident with an inactive vertex is counted once in
the sum of the lower bounds (2); an active-active constant edge is counted
twice.  There are no inactive-inactive edges.  Comparing with (1), whose
remaining degree summands are nonnegative, forces: no active-active
constant edge, equality in every (2), and degree one at every nonconstant
edge.  Equality also forces `g=0,q=r_i+1` at an active vertex.  This proves
(3)--(5).

## Geometric consequence and remaining gap

Distinct inactive components are disjoint embedded affine lines.  If
there are at least two, rectifying one to `u=0` shows that every other is
a parallel line `u=alpha_i`: the restriction of `u` to a disjoint `A1`
is a nowhere-zero unit and hence constant.

For a rectangular arrangement this common coordinate, together with
Riemann--Hurwitz on the active component curves, produces the global
`A2`-fibration and proves cancellation (see `snc_constant_nodes.md`).
For a general SNC embedding, active components not meeting enough
inactive lines need not be fibers or sections of one polynomial ruling.
The orientation proposition alone therefore does not yet trivialize the
quotient; the missing datum is a global ruling compatible with all active
components, not another Euler or divisor-lattice inequality.
